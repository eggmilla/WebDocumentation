Iterative Process

I took inspiration from government sites such as my aged care and Victoria's ombudsman as they are rated as highly accessible. Drawing upon their similarities, I discoverd that they:

  • use colour blocking
  • have lots of white space
  • use only one typeface with different weights and sizes
  • have links or buttons to reveal more information
I wanted to take these into account when creating my own website in order to be more inclusive. The initial changes I made were:
  • using two typefaces
  • not using a cow print pattern background as it could be very distracting even if static
  • creating obvious hover states for all links - used underline and colour change for text
  • creating more white space

From retail sites such as zero co and ad guard I saw the use of animation and call-to-actions and was inspired to possibly include some of these on my site. I included a 'donate now' button on the landing page as the header doesn't feature an explicit link to the donation page. I couldn't find a way to include animations in my final assignment but I think they could be a space for further development.

Initial Design

Two different typefaces for main text and navigation, title and footer text.

4 square images as shortcut links

Additionally, when performing casual user feedback I was told:

Points #4 and #5 are referring to the footer as the social media favicons were spaced across the entire bottom of the footer.

And in combination with this feedback and reflecting on my site's main purpose, I made further changes:

  • having a slightly see-through hero textbox
  • used only one typeface to keep content looking consistent
  • using round borders on images to create more white space
  • pushing all the social media links in the footer to one side for convenience and better spacing
  • creating a thinner footer to not "squash" the page when both header and footer are visible
  • dimming images to brighten them when hovered over for obvious image hover states
  • having a consistent look for all images (which are also links) - round, dimmed and with caption underneath
  • omitted the "learn more" shortcut image from the landing page as it wouldn't be considered on the main purposes of the site and it is featured in the header

Second Design

One typeface across whole site

3 images with round borders for white space + lower brightness for more obvious hover states

Third Design

Changed the colour scheme of the website as I received a lot of feedback on the lack of colour on content pages.

Put a gradient background behind the donation form to draw more attention to it.

Added in breadcrumb navigation (forgot to code it in earlier) and it also added a nice extra pop of colour on the page!
